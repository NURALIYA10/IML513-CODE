Slip Knot

A slip knot is the starting point for most crochet projects. To make one, create a loop with the yarn, pull a strand through, and gently tighten it on the hook.

Chain (ch)

After the slip knot, you create a series of loops (chains) by wrapping yarn over the hook and pulling it through the loop. It looks like a braided line and is used to start rows and create space.

Magic Ring

The magic ring is a technique used to start projects worked in the round, like hats. Once your first round of stitches is made, you pull the yarn tail to tighten the loop, leaving no hole in the center.

Single Crochet (sc)

This is one of the most basic crochet stitches. Insert the hook into the stitch, yarn over, pull through, yarn over again, and pull through both loops on the hook.

Half Double Crochet (hdc)

Double Crochet (dc)

Yarn over, insert hook, yarn over and pull through, yarn over and pull through two loops twice. It creates a taller stitch used in many patterns.

Slip Stitch (sl st)

Used to join rounds or move across stitches. Insert hook, yarn over and pull through both the stitch and the loop on the hook in one motion.

Increase

An increase adds stitches by working more than one stitch into the same space or stitch. It's used to shape projects.

Decrease

A decrease reduces stitches by combining two stitches into one. It's useful for shaping and tapering crochet pieces.
